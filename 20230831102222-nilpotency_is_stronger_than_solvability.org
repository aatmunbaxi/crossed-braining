#+title: nilpotency is stronger than solvability
#+filetags: :default:
#+last_modified: [2023-08-31 Thu 10:22]
#+setupfile: ~/Documents/org/latex_template.org
A [[id:a071ca4e-e692-4487-855f-5dc3c6bf8190][nilpotent Lie algebra]] is always [[id:5505cff6-4c69-48c0-9f20-cfd528dbf5b4][solvable]], since \( \mathfrak{g} ^{(n)} \subset \mathfrak{g} ^n  \).

The converse is not true; the algebra of upper triangular matrices \( \mathfrak{t} (n,k) \) is solvable but not nilpotent.
