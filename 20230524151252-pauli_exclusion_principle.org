#+title: pauli exclusion principle

The Pauli exclusion principle roughly states that two or more identical [[id:737d21d5-5774-46d0-8415-00dac1c3cad6][fermion]]s cannot occupy the same quantum state simultaneously.
Rigorously, if \( \Psi \) is a wave function, and \( x,y \) are two identical fermions (same spin), then \( \Psi(x,y) = - \Psi(y,x) \).
