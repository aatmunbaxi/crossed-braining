#+title: string theory and fermions
#+filetags: :physics:particle-physics:quantum-mechanics:string-theory:
#+last_modified: [2023-05-24 Wed 15:04]
#+setupfile: ~/Documents/org/latex_template.org
Bosonic string theory, with the Nambu-Goto action on worldsheets, does not predict the existence of fermions.
To predict the fermionic string, we need a dual notion of the NG action, given on [[id:9ea86d6a-457b-4841-aa79-266206c71e2f][spinor]] fields.

Recall that spinors are elements of a representation of a [[id:fd998f12-5b30-436a-a8db-3db2a8902d52][Clifford algebra]], which [[id:515d597f-2549-483c-80c0-aaa8bd65630f][are quantizations of exterior algebras]].
This is similar to the notion of the [[id:30fd7d0e-8047-41f2-b3f5-afb34e2348af][Pauli exclusion principle]] of [[id:737d21d5-5774-46d0-8415-00dac1c3cad6][fermions]].
The exterior algebra is antisymmetric, much like identical fermions swap the sign of wave functions.
