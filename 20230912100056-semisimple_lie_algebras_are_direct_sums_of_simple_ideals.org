#+title: semisimple Lie algebras are direct sums of simple ideals
#+last_modified: [2023-09-12 Tue 10:00]
#+setupfile: ~/Documents/org/latex_template.org
Every finite dimensional semisimple [[id:04c2e796-c1db-4e7b-8317-6409312fcba6][Lie algebra]] \( \mathfrak{g}  \) decomposes as
\begin{equation}
\mathfrak{g}  = \bigoplus_{i=1}^{n} \mathfrak{a} _i
\end{equation}
such that \([\mathfrak{a} _i , \mathfrak{a}_j ]=0\) for \( i \neq j \).
Moreover every simple ideal of \( \mathfrak{g}  \) is one of \( \mathfrak{a} _i \), so the splitting is unique.
The Killing form will be the restrictions to each ideal.

This is an equivalent characterization of semisimplicity, alternative to the definition that \( \operatorname{Rad} \mathfrak{g} =0 \).
